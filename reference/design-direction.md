# Design Reference — Competitor/Sibling Sites

> These three Naturally Network chapter sites represent the design direction the client wants. The new Naturally San Diego site should feel modern and community-forward like these, not like the outdated current WordPress site.

---

## Client Feedback on Current Site
- Current site "feels cringe"
- Wants fresh, modern, community-driven feel
- Should represent the brand correctly without being dated or generic
- **Specific favorite among the three has not been identified yet** — follow up with client

---

## Reference Site 1: Naturally New York
**URL**: https://www.naturallynewyork.org/
**Platform**: Squarespace

### What Works
- High-energy carousel hero with strong community photography
- Bold "New York is..." messaging pattern (localized identity)
- Social media feed integration ("Follow Our Journey" Instagram grid)
- "The Buzz" section for current news and announcements
- Podcast feature section
- Clean mission statement block
- Newsletter signup at bottom
- Strong CTAs: "Become a Member" / "Become a Partner"

### Navigation Pattern
- Team, Partners (dropdown), Programming (dropdown), Resources (dropdown)
- Persistent "Become a Member" CTA button

### Content Sections (Homepage)
1. Hero carousel with community photos + messaging
2. Mission/vision statement
3. "Who We Are" block
4. "Join Our Community" CTA section
5. "The Buzz" — current news/announcements
6. Social media feed grid
7. Newsletter signup
8. Footer

## Reference Site 2: Naturally NorCal
**URL**: https://naturallynorcal.org/
**Platform**: Squarespace

### What Works
- Clean, professional layout with strong visual hierarchy
- Community stats prominently displayed (800+ Members, 49 Partners, 2,800+ Event Attendees, 44 Events)
- "What We Do" visual grid (Naturally Rising, Education, Forums, Networking, Retail Access, Naturally Network)
- Community Spotlight section with real quotes from leaders
- Founder's Circle sponsor logos in a clean scrolling row
- "Community Buzz" testimonials carousel
- "Join the Community" section with multiple event photos
- Titanium & Platinum tier sponsor display
- GlueUp member login in nav

### Navigation Pattern
- About Us (dropdown), Our Community (dropdown), Programs & Events (dropdown)
- Member Login link
- No persistent CTA button — CTAs embedded in page sections

### Content Sections (Homepage)
1. Hero carousel (community imagery + messaging)
2. Mission tagline with icon
3. Founder's Circle sponsor logos
4. Featured Events
5. Community stats (large numbers)
6. "What We Do" visual grid
7. Community Spotlight quotes
8. Join the Community CTA with event photos
9. Community Buzz testimonials
10. Titanium & Platinum sponsors
11. Footer with links

### Standout Elements
- Stats section is very effective — gives immediate credibility
- Visual grid for programs is engaging and scannable
- Multiple layers of social proof (spotlight + buzz + sponsor logos)

## Reference Site 3: Naturally Colorado
**URL**: https://www.naturallycolorado.org/
**Platform**: Squarespace

### What Works
- Strong storytelling — rich history section (founded 2005, evolution from Boulder)
- Testimonials throughout the site from real community members
- "Naturally Rising" pitch competition prominently featured
- Donation/fundraising CTA ($50,000 goal)
- Forums and Office Hours programs highlighted
- Member Code of Ethics — adds credibility
- Slack community integration
- Resource Center with past webinar archives
- 1,000+ members, 100 sponsors — stats used for credibility

### Navigation Pattern
- Similar dropdown structure to NorCal
- Membership, Sponsorship, Events/Programs, Resources

### Content Approach
- More text-heavy and story-driven than the other two
- Emphasizes heritage and community roots
- Strong testimonial culture — quotes from members throughout
- Clear membership benefits breakdown with tiers

## Design Direction Summary (For Claude Code Prompt)

### Layout Principles
- Full-bleed hero sections with community photography
- Generous whitespace and breathing room between sections
- Clean section transitions (no boxy WordPress feel)
- Visual hierarchy through typography scale, not decoration
- Mobile-first, responsive throughout

### Section Patterns to Include
- Hero with community photo + bold headline + CTA
- Mission/identity statement block
- Community stats (members, sponsors, events, attendees)
- Sponsor logos display (tiered)
- "What We Do" or programs visual grid
- Community testimonials/spotlight
- Upcoming events section
- Newsletter signup
- Strong CTAs for membership and sponsorship throughout

### Photography Style
- Real community photos (events, networking, people connecting)
- Warm, candid, not stock-photo feeling
- Full-bleed and half-bleed image treatments

### Typography Feel
- Clean, modern sans-serif (Poppins per brand guide)
- Large headlines with clear hierarchy
- Readable body text with good line height

### Color Application
- Green palette as primary (per brand guide)
- White/light backgrounds for content sections
- Dark green or black for contrast sections
- Accent colors (teal, red-orange) for CTAs and highlights
