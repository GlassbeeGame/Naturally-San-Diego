# Content Strategy — Naturally San Diego

> How content should be sourced, written, and styled across the site.

---

## Content Source

The current website (https://naturallysandiego.org) serves as the **content skeleton**. Use it for:
- Factual information (mission, who they are, what they do)
- Existing sponsor names and relationships
- Leadership team details
- Event types and descriptions
- Membership information
- 1% for the Planet partnership

**Do NOT copy the current site's writing style.** The copy needs to be completely rewritten.

---

## Voice & Tone (From Brand Guide)

### The Voice Is:
- Approachable, friendly, casual
- Open, inclusive, engaging
- Credible & trustworthy
- Entrepreneurial
- Upbeat, progressive, dynamic
- Inspirational, optimistic
- Supportive, collaborative

### The Voice Is NOT:
- Corporate or formal
- Sterile or serious
- Heavy handed or intellectual
- Overly trendy or overly wholesome
- Hierarchical or exclusive

### In Practice:
- Write like a smart, passionate community leader — not a nonprofit brochure
- Short, punchy sentences mixed with longer explanatory ones
- Direct address ("you" / "your") to make it personal
- Confident but warm — not salesy, not stiff
- Celebrate the community, don't just describe it
- Avoid jargon unless it's natural to the CPG/natural products audience

---

## Copy Approach by Section

### Hero / Homepage
- Bold, identity-driven headline (similar to "New York is the Heart of Natural Products")
- Localize to San Diego — leverage the city's identity
- Short supporting line + clear CTA

### About / Our Story
- Rewrite from current site content
- Tell the story of NSD within the larger Naturally Network
- Highlight what makes the San Diego chapter unique

### Leadership
- Clean bios — name, title, short bio, headshot
- Source from current site, update if needed

### Programs & Events
- Energetic, action-oriented descriptions
- Emphasize what attendees get out of it
- Signature events (Naturally Rising, Fall Fling) get richer descriptions

### Membership
- Benefits-first approach — lead with what members get
- Clear tier breakdown
- FAQs should address real objections/questions

### Sponsorship
- Value-driven pitch — why sponsoring NSD matters
- Package breakdown with clear differentiation
- Easy path to inquiry (form)

### Resources
- Brief, functional descriptions
- Job Board, CPG Academy, Member Wins, Slack — each gets a card or section
