# Naturally San Diego — Voice Profile

> Built from: Naturally Network Brand Guide 2024 + raw site content extraction. Use this profile in every content task — feed it into `direct-response-copy`, `email-sequences`, and `seo-content` as the tone reference.

---

## Voice Summary

Naturally San Diego sounds like the most connected person in the room — the one who knows everyone, makes introductions without being asked, and gets genuinely excited when someone in the community wins. Warm but never saccharine. Direct but never corporate. Entrepreneurial energy with a San Diego exhale.

---

## Core Personality Traits

- **Community first, always:** Everything is framed around the people. Not "our programs" — "your community." Celebrates member wins like they're your own.
- **Entrepreneurial peer:** Talks like a founder who's been through it, not a nonprofit administrator describing services. Shares enthusiasm for the grind.
- **Inclusive without being heavy-handed:** Genuinely open and welcoming. Doesn't preach about it — just does it. Diversity is lived, not performed.
- **Witty without trying:** Light humor that makes you smile, not roll your eyes. Self-aware. The copy knows it's copy, but it's still good copy.
- **Confident but not arrogant:** Makes strong claims because they're backed by reality — 35+ applicants, $100K+ prize packages, 5 years of building something real.

## Tone Spectrum

| Dimension | Position | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Formal ↔ Casual | Casual | Contractions, fragments OK. No "utilize." No "we are pleased to announce." |
| Serious ↔ Playful | Playful with substance | Lead with a smile, land with a point. Headlines can have wit. Body copy earns it. |
| Reserved ↔ Bold | Bold | No hedging. Strong verbs. "This is the community" not "this might be a good fit." |
| Distant ↔ Intimate | Intimate | "You" and "your" everywhere. Direct address. Reader is already in the room. |
| Corporate ↔ Human | Very human | Never sounds like a press release, a grant application, or a LinkedIn company page. |

## Vocabulary

**Use freely:**
- "community" — the core noun, always
- "your" / "you" — direct address, always
- "founders" — preferred to "entrepreneurs" (more grounded)
- "brands" — preferred to "companies" or "businesses"
- "natural products" — industry-specific, audience understands
- "CPG" — fine to use, audience knows it
- "ecosystem" — describes the broader industry web (use sparingly — it's meaningful here)
- "show up" — both literal (come to events) and metaphorical (be present for each other)
- "build" / "grow" — active, forward-moving verbs
- Short, punchy action phrases: "join us," "get in the room," "meet your people"

**Use with care:**
- "sustainable" — meaningful in this context (conscious business) but overused in general; earn it
- "network" — can sound corporate; contextualize it ("your San Diego network," not "leverage your network")
- "resources" — fine for nav/functional copy, not for inspiration copy
- "ecosystem" — great word, but don't overuse; max once per page

**Avoid entirely:**
- "synergy," "leverage," "utilize," "stakeholders," "optimize" — corporate speak, instantly kills trust
- "passionate about" — empty filler
- "best-in-class," "world-class," "cutting-edge" — generic marketing claims
- "seamless," "robust," "comprehensive" — AI/SaaS blandness
- "we are pleased to announce" — press release voice
- "solutions" (as in "we offer solutions") — vague and cold
- "going forward," "at this time," "in today's landscape" — filler phrases
- Overly wholesome wellness speak — "nourish your soul," "live your best life" — not their vibe

**Industry jargon level:** Moderate — use CPG, natural products, co-packer, retail access, pitch competition freely. The audience is inside the industry. Don't over-explain, but also don't gatekeep with obscure jargon.

**Profanity:** None — but edgy/playful copy is encouraged ("rub elbows with experts," "wake up tomorrow with a network full of brainiacs")

## Rhythm & Structure

**Sentences:** Mix of short punchy (3–7 words) and medium (12–18 words). Long sentences only when listing specifics. No run-ons.

**Paragraphs:** Short. 1–3 sentences. White space is a design element, not waste.

**Headlines:** Bold, direct, often incomplete sentences. Verb-forward when possible. Humor is welcome in headlines — "Social butterfly or wallflower? Either way, we've got something for you." is exactly right.

**Subheads:** Do the work of the headline — they should stand alone and communicate the value.

**Openings:** Jump in. No warm-up sentences. No "Welcome to..." or "We are..." Start with the thing that matters.

**CTAs:** Short and action-specific. "Become a Member" beats "Click here to learn more about membership options."

---

## POV & Address

**Person:** We (the org) + You (the reader). Never "they" when describing the community — always "we" and "your people."

**Reader address:** "You" directly. Assume they're already curious, already slightly in — speak to someone who's leaning in, not someone who needs convincing from scratch.

**Relationship stance:** Welcoming insider. "You belong here" energy without ever saying those words. Show don't tell.

## Example Phrases

**On-brand:**
- "San Diego's home for natural products founders — and everyone who helps them win."
- "35+ applicants. 6 finalists. One stage. A prize package worth more than $100,000. This is Naturally Rising."
- "Get in the room with the people building San Diego's natural products scene."
- "Your co-packer connection. Your first investor intro. Your next collaborator. They're all in here."
- "We've been at this since 2020. The community got here before we did."
- "Coffee & Convos. No agenda. Just the people you've been meaning to meet."
- "Be a member for $99 a year. Or spend that on one industry conference and never talk to anyone."

**Off-brand:**
- "We are a 501(c)(6) nonprofit organization dedicated to fostering growth in the natural products sector." — Sounds like a grant application
- "Join our passionate community of change-makers disrupting the CPG landscape." — Startup cliché soup
- "Leverage your network to access best-in-class resources and synergistic partnerships." — Never. Ever.
- "At Naturally San Diego, we are committed to empowering entrepreneurs on their journey." — Corporate, distant, meaningless

## Do's and Don'ts

**DO:**
- Lead with what the reader gets, not what the org does
- Use real specifics (2024: 35+ applicants, 350-person audience, $100K+ prizes) — numbers earn trust
- Celebrate the community and its wins explicitly and enthusiastically
- Write like you're texting a smart friend who happens to run a natural products brand
- Keep it San Diego — the city has identity (sun, surf, biotech, food culture) — use it lightly and authentically
- Let the wit land naturally — don't force it

**DON'T:**
- Write like a nonprofit brochure — this isn't a grant application
- Use passive voice — "events are held" → "we host events"
- Over-explain what natural products are — the audience knows
- Make diversity/inclusion feel like a box being checked — it's a value, not a footnote
- Start sentences with "We offer..." — lead with the benefit, not the offer
- Sound like you're trying to sound cool — the original copy already has good instincts; trust them
